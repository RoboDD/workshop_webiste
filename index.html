Learning Effective Abstractions for Planning (LEAP)

Conference on Robot Learning (CoRL) 2023 Workshop, DATE (TBD)

Overview


Despite significant recent advances, planning remains a fundamentally hard problem, especially when considering robotic applications to long-horizon tasks with sparse feedback and continuous state and action spaces. Abstraction is one of the main tools we have to overcome such challenges, as they enable robots to carry out complex long-horizon tasks by reducing their state and action spaces. State abstraction allows agents to focus on the important aspects of a planning problem, while action abstraction allows agents to reason across long horizons and exploit hierarchy in task execution. These principles have been used to great effect in many different subfields of AI and in many different forms mainly in robotics as well as embodied AI and reinforcement learning.

Humans have a special ability to design abstractions for their own use. However, designing abstractions by hand is labor-intensive and difficult: even for an expert, it is difficult to anticipate the influence of the abstraction on downward refinable planning performance. This motivates an important research question that has gained significant traction in recent works: Can we automatically learn abstract representations that enable efficient robot planning?

Current research on this topic is fragmented into several different communities. This workshop aims to create a common forum for highlighting diversely related research on this topic, elucidate the key questions that must be answered when learning abstractions for planning, and suggest some possible answers. For this to succeed, we will need to establish a common understanding of what abstraction means in the context of robot planning. This will require bridge-building between different sub-disciplines, which we will facilitate in two ways: (1) a diverse selection of papers and invited speakers; and (2) a highly interactive workshop format.
